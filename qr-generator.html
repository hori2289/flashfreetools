QR Code Generator

Create a QR code from any URL or text, then download it as a PNG.

How to create a QR code

Type or paste any URL or text into the input field, then click Generate QR Code. Your QR code appears immediately. Click Download PNG to save the image to your device. The downloaded file is ready to use in print materials, presentations, or social media posts.

What you can encode in a QR code

A QR code can store any text up to several hundred characters. Common uses include website URLs, contact information (vCard), plain text messages, email addresses, phone numbers, Wi-Fi network credentials, and app store links. Keep the text short for better scan reliability on small printed sizes.

Popular use cases

Business cards: Add a QR code that links to your website or LinkedIn profile so contacts can find you instantly with a phone scan.

Restaurant menus: Link to a digital menu so customers can view it on their phone without handling a physical menu.

Wi-Fi sharing: Encode your network name and password so guests can join without typing credentials. Format the text as: WIFI:S:NetworkName;T:WPA;P:Password;;

Event check-in: Link to a registration or RSVP page for quick mobile access at the door.

Product packaging: Link to instructions, warranty registration, or a product landing page directly from the box.

Print to digital: Add a QR code to flyers, posters, or brochures so readers can visit your website without typing a long URL.

Tips for best results

Use short URLs or a link shortener to make QR codes less dense and easier to scan. Print at a minimum of 2 by 2 centimeters. Test the QR code with multiple devices before printing. Add a short label beneath the code such as "Scan to visit our website" to encourage people to use it.

Frequently asked questions

Do QR codes expire?
No. A QR code encodes your content directly and will always decode to the same text. If you linked to a URL, the QR code works as long as that webpage is still online.
Is there a character limit?
QR codes can technically store up to around 4,000 characters, but for reliable scanning keep content under 200 characters. Shorter content produces a simpler pattern that scans faster.
Can any phone scan QR codes?
Yes. Most modern smartphones can scan QR codes using the built-in camera app without needing a separate app. Open your camera, point it at the QR code, and tap the notification that appears.
Is the QR code stored on your servers?
No. The QR code is generated entirely in your browser using JavaScript. Nothing is uploaded or saved anywhere.
